Kaju Katli

Kaju katli (literally "cashew slice"), also known as kaju barfi, is an Indian dessert, originating in the Deccan
and is popularly consumed throughout north India. Kaju means cashew; barfi is often made by
thickening milk with sugar and other ingredients (such as dry fruits and mild spices). Kesar kaju katli includes saffron.
It is similar to a Barfi.

Ingredients for Kaju Katli

1}Cashew Nuts (Kaju): 1 cup
2}Sugar: 1/2 cup
3}Water: 1/4 cup
4}Ghee (clarified butter): 1 teaspoon (for greasing)
5}Cardamom Powder: 1/4 teaspoon (optional, for flavor)
6}Rose Water or Kewra Water: 1/2 teaspoon (optional, for aroma)
7}Silver Vark (edible silver leaf): for garnishing (optional)

Preparation Steps:

-Prepare Cashew Powder:

#Dry the cashew nuts by spreading them on a plate for a few hours or lightly roasting them for a few minutes to remove any moisture.
#Once dry, grind the cashews to a fine powder in a blender or food processor.
#Be careful not to over-process, as this can release oil and turn the cashews into a paste.

-Make Sugar Syrup:

#In a heavy-bottomed pan, combine the sugar and water.
# Heat the mixture on medium flame, stirring continuously, until the sugar dissolves completely.
# Continue to cook the syrup until it reaches a one-string consistency.
#This means that when you take a small amount of the syrup between your thumb and index finger, it should form a single thread when the fingers are pulled apart.

-Combine Cashew Powder and Syrup:

#Reduce the heat to low and add the cashew powder to the sugar syrup.
#Mix well and cook on low heat, stirring continuously, until the mixture starts to thicken and leaves the sides of the pan.
#This should take about 5-7 minutes.
#Add cardamom powder and rose water or kewra water, if using, and mix well.

-Knead the Dough:

#Remove the mixture from the heat and let it cool slightly until it's warm enough to handle.
#Grease your hands with a little ghee and knead the mixture into a smooth dough.
# If the mixture is too sticky, you can add a little more ghee.

-Roll Out the Dough:

#Place the dough between two sheets of parchment paper or on a greased surface.
#Roll it out with a rolling pin to about 1/4 inch thickness

-Garnish and Cut:

#If using silver vark, gently place it on top of the rolled-out dough.
#Let it cool completely, then cut the dough into diamond-shaped pieces using a sharp knife.

-serve:

#Once completely cooled, store the Kaju Katli in an airtight container.
#It can be kept at room temperature for several days.
